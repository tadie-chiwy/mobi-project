The Mobi is Zimbabwe's very first
Mobile Soup Kitchen, providing
nutritious meals and support to
woman and children in some of
our most vulnerable and isolated
communities.

The MOBI was launched in October 2021 and already providing
its beneficiaries with crucial access to nutrition , support ,
education , healthcare and long term change.

THE MOBI

The MOBI currently provides in excess of 1,200 nutrious meals per month to children under 15 living in squarer camps in Hatcliffe.We believe the most effective way of
helping others is to understand their plight and gain their trust before forcing solutions onto them.The best way to achieve this is to spend time within specific communities,
developing a relationship with them whilst assisting where we can .We strongly believe that , through the use of the MOBI , we will be able to develop this relationship and provide long term assistance to the people to assistance to the people that we visit.

The Mobi is also equipped to react to crises involving at risk communities and provide disaster relief where needed and is a fixture at all Harare's fairs, exhibitions and markets where it is used as a platform to raise awareness, fundraise and sell crafts,curios and artwork on behalf of our vulnerable communities.

AIMS OF THE MOBI

With the MOBI we are able to reach communities that have no access to alternative
assistance through the various soup kitchens, Churches, and Chariables organisations
operating in and around Harare, with a forcus on children under the age of 15.

The Mobi currently visits two major squatter camps in Hatcliffe each week, initially visiting
the same communities in order to buld up the important relationships required for
effective change.As our team buld up registers of our beneficiaries , we will begin to
malnutrition, literacy and education , healthcare and general welfare , as well as
HIV/AIDS treatment , support and education.

As the MOBI continues to grow , we will expand our reach into other communities such as
Epworth and Hopely.
